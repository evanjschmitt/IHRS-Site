Adopting A Rabbit

The IHRS places rabbits in homes where:

  • The rabbit is the responsibility of an adult
  • The rabbit lives indoors as part of the family
  • The rabbit receives a diet of limited pellets, unlimited hay, and vegetables
  • The rabbit receives regular veterinary care to maintain good health
  • The rabbit is returned to Indiana House Rabbit Society should the family no longer be able to care for the rabbit

Our Adoption Process

  1. Submit our Adoption Questionnaire
  2. Participate in a Zoom consultation
  3. Get supplies for your rabbit, or purchase a full setup from the IHRS!
  4. Arrange a time for a matchmaking session
  5. Sign the adoption contract
  6. Pay the adoption fee

Adoption Fee

The adoption fee is $100 for a single rabbit, and $175 for a bonded pair of rabbits.

This fee includes:

  • Rabbit spay/neuter surgery and any necessary vet care
  • One small bag of hay
  • One small bag of pellets
  • Indiana House Rabbit Society as an ongoing resource during your rabbit's life.

Finding a Mate for Your Rabbit

  1. Submit our Adoption Questionnaire
  2. Participate in a Zoom consultation
  3. Arrange a time to attend a bunny date

    (IHRS Volunteers will help introduce your bunny to the other bunnies, and help find a good match)

  4. Sign the adoption contract
  5. Pay the adoption fee

Our Adoptables

IHRS Adoptable Rabbits are:

  • Rescued from Shelters/Abandoned strays rescued from certain death
  • Screened by a veterinarian at the time of rescue and treated for any illness
  • Spayed or neutered prior to placement
  • Housed in foster homes and socialized by family members
  • Litterbox trained
  • Given a minimum 14 hours of supervised exercise per week
  • Evaluated for temperament to ensure the best match in their adoptive home
